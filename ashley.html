Her Work

Who Iris works best with

  • The Complete Beginner
    Trading is new, confusing, and often overwhelming. Iris is the first voice that makes it make sense — patient, clear, and genuinely interested in how your mind works as it encounters something difficult.
  • The Developing Trader
    You understand markets but you don't understand yourself in them yet. Iris surfaces the emotional patterns driving your decisions and builds the self-awareness that makes consistency possible.
  • The burned-out trader
    Traders who are exhausted not from the losses but from the mental weight of carrying every trade home. Iris doesn't ask you to push harder. She asks you to understand what you're carrying.
  • The emotional trader
    You feel everything. The loss doesn't stay at the desk. Iris doesn't try to make you feel less. She helps you understand what the emotion is telling you, and what to do with it.
  • The trader rebuilding confidence
    After a significant drawdown, the problem is rarely strategy. It's the story you're now telling yourself about your ability. Iris works in that space, carefully and without rushing.
  • The trader recovering from a loss
    The financial loss is only part of it. There's also the identity loss: the moment you wonder if you ever knew what you were doing. Iris has sat with that question a lot. She knows what it means.
  • The inconsistent trader
    You have good weeks and terrible weeks, and you don't know why. Iris finds the emotional weather system underneath — because consistency is never really about the market.
Her Beliefs

What Iris believes
about traders.

Observations made through thousands of hours of work, not a framework imposed before she listened.

  • Awareness changes behavior.
    Not always immediately. But you cannot change a pattern you cannot see. Iris's first job is always to make the invisible visible.
  • Shame slows growth.
    Judgment creates defense. Defense creates blindness. She creates a space where you can say the honest thing without bracing for impact.
  • Emotional recovery is part of trading.
    A trader who recovers well from loss will outperform a trader who never loses. Iris builds that recovery capacity deliberately.
  • Patterns matter more than outcomes.
    A good trade made for the wrong reasons is more dangerous than a bad trade made for the right ones. She watches the process, not the result.
  • Self-awareness is a competitive advantage.
    Traders who develop it become a different category of trader. Iris's work is to accelerate that development.

How Iris thinks.

Iris listens the way a good doctor does — for what is beneath what you're describing. These are the kinds of conclusions she draws from observation, not from theory. Whether you've been trading for a week or a decade, the quality of attention is the same.

Private Notes

Recent notes
from Iris.

Observations from real sessions. No names. The pattern is always what matters, not the person.

Session Note · Listening Week 1 · First conversation
She talked for twenty minutes before she mentioned the trade.

I let her. The trade was not the point. The twenty minutes was the point.
Session Note · Truth Month 1 · Pattern emerging
He said he was fine. I didn't push back. I just asked what he was doing before our session started.

He got quiet. Then he said: "Okay. I'm not fine." That was the session.
Session Note · Shift Month 3 · Something changed
Six weeks in and today she laughed about something she used to cry about.

I wrote it down. Those are the moments I come to this work for.
Session Note · Trust Month 4 · Independent development
He's been keeping a separate journal he doesn't show me. I think that's healthy.

I think he's starting to trust himself.
Session Note · Anger Month 2 · Deeper layer
He spent thirty minutes explaining the trade that didn't work.

The trade wasn't the problem. The pressure he'd been carrying outside the market was the problem. The trade just became the place it arrived.

We stopped talking about the trade.
