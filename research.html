Research
Division

Ongoing behavioral research programs studying trader development at the psychological, identity, and performance layers.

RP-01
The Discipline-Performance Gap
Ongoing

Examining why disciplined rule-adherence does not automatically translate to improved P&L outcomes in the short term, and what the latency curve actually looks like across trader development stages.

RP-02
Identity Transition Latency
Ongoing

Measuring how long it takes for a trader who has demonstrably changed their behavior to internalize that change as a stable identity belief — and what shortens or extends that window.

RP-03
Loss Sequence Psychology
Ongoing

Behavioral patterns in traders who experience three or more consecutive losses: what separates recovery within 48 hours from spirals that persist into the following week.

RP-04
Rule Adherence Decay
Ongoing

How rule adherence rate changes over time when no external review mechanism is in place, and what types of accountability structures most effectively slow the decay.

RP-05
Recovery Architecture
Active Enrollment

Structural differences between traders who recover quickly from significant drawdowns versus those who compound their losses — focused on the first 72 hours after a session that triggers the Guardian Layer.

EdgeKeeper members contribute behavioral data to these programs through their daily practice. Their data — anonymized — forms the evidence base.
