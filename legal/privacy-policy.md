## 2. What Data We Collect

### 2.1 Account Data
When you register, we collect:
- Your email address
- Your password (stored in hashed, encrypted form — we cannot read it)
- Your name or display name (if provided)
- Account creation date and login timestamps

### 2.2 Onboarding and Profile Data
When you complete onboarding, we collect:
- Trading experience level and background (e.g., prop firm trader, retail trader)
- Self-reported trading weaknesses, goals, and patterns
- Psychological self-assessment responses (e.g., stress responses, discipline challenges)
- Preferred AI mentor selection
- Any other profile information you voluntarily provide

This data is used to personalize your coaching experience. You are not required to provide it with perfect accuracy — it shapes AI responses but does not affect any financial decision.

### 2.3 Conversation Data (Text)
All text conversations you have with Mike and Ashley are stored, including:
- Your messages
- AI-generated responses
- Timestamps
- Session metadata

We store this to provide continuity across sessions, allow you to review past conversations, and improve the service.

### 2.4 Voice Data
If you use voice features, we process:
- Your voice recordings during voice sessions
- Transcripts of voice sessions generated from those recordings

Voice recordings are processed by ElevenLabs (our voice AI provider) and may be stored temporarily by them for processing. See Section 6 for third-party details.

**We do not store raw voice recordings long-term on our own servers.** Transcripts from voice sessions are stored in our system and treated the same as text conversation data.

### 2.5 Journal Entries
If you use the journaling feature, we store all journal entries you create, including text content and timestamps.

### 2.6 Payment Data
We collect:
- Transaction records (amount, date, subscription tier, status)
- Your billing email address

We do **not** collect or store your card number, bank account details, or any raw payment instrument data. This is handled entirely by Polar.sh. See Section 6.

### 2.7 Usage and Technical Data
We automatically collect:
- IP address
- Device type and operating system
- Browser type and version
- Pages visited and features used within EdgeKeeper
- Session duration and frequency
- Error logs

This data is used for service operation, security, and product improvement.

### 2.8 Communications
If you contact our support team, we retain those communications.

We use your data to:

- Create and manage your account
- Provide the AI mentorship service, including personalizing Mike and Ashley's responses based on your profile

- Improve the product through aggregated, anonymized analytics
- Detect and prevent fraud, abuse, and security threats

We do **not:**

- Send you unsolicited marketing from third parties
- Use your data to make automated decisions that have significant legal or similarly significant effects on you

> **LAWYER REVIEW FLAGS — Do not go live without resolving these:**
>
> 1. **NDPA compliance:** The Nigeria Data Protection Act 2023 introduced new requirements including Data Protection Officers (DPO) for certain processors. A Nigerian data protection lawyer should confirm whether EdgeKeeper is required to appoint a DPO and register with the NDPC.
>
> 2. **NDPR Data Controller registration:** Under NDPR, organizations above certain thresholds are required to register as data controllers. Verify your registration obligations with the NDPC.
>
> 3. **GDPR adequacy for EU users:** Nigeria does not currently have an EU adequacy decision. If you have significant EU users, you may need Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) and a Transfer Impact Assessment for data flowing to your Nigerian servers. Have an EU data protection lawyer review this.
>
> 4. **UK GDPR:** Post-Brexit, UK users are subject to UK GDPR, not EU GDPR. The transfer safeguard requirements differ. If you have UK users, confirm compliance with a UK-qualified solicitor.
>
> 5. **Supabase data region:** Insert the actual Supabase region you use. This is material to transfer compliance.
>
> 6. **OpenAI training data terms:** OpenAI's API terms can change. Verify the current data processing terms and whether you need a Data Processing Agreement (DPA) with OpenAI for GDPR purposes.
>
> 7. **Voice data — GDPR Article 9:** Voice data may constitute biometric data under GDPR, which is a special category requiring explicit consent and heightened protections. If you have EEA users, have a GDPR lawyer review your voice data handling.
>
> 8. **CCPA (California):** If you have California users, you may have obligations under CCPA/CPRA including the right to opt out of "sale" of personal information (even if you don't sell data, the definition is broad). Have a US lawyer assess.
